Quito, Ecuador

Quito, the capital of Ecuador, was founded on December 6, 1534, on the ruins of an Inca city and stands at an altitude of approximately 2,850 meters above sea level, making it one of the highest capitals in the world. Its historic center, one of the largest and best preserved in the Americas, was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1978, sharing that honor with Krakow. It has a population of around 1.76 million in the city and nearly 2.7 million in its metropolitan area, making it the second most populous city in the country.
